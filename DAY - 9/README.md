## ğŸ§  Key Concepts Covered

### ğŸ”¹ Security Alerts
Security alerts are notifications generated by security tools when suspicious activity is detected.

Alerts may originate from:
- SIEM systems  
- Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR) tools  
- Firewalls and IDS/IPS  
- Authentication and access logs  

Not all alerts represent real attacks.

---

### ğŸ”¹ Alert Fatigue
Alert fatigue occurs when analysts are overwhelmed by excessive alerts, many of which may be false positives.

Consequences include:
- Missed real incidents  
- Delayed response times  
- Reduced analyst efficiency  

Effective triage is essential to combat alert fatigue.

---

### ğŸ”¹ Alert Triage
Alert triage is the process of:
- Reviewing alerts  
- Determining their legitimacy  
- Assigning severity levels  
- Deciding whether escalation is required  

Triage helps SOC teams focus on **high-impact threats first**.

---

### ğŸ”¹ Incident Prioritization
Incidents are prioritized based on:
- Severity and impact  
- Affected systems or users  
- Likelihood of exploitation  
- Business criticality  

Prioritization ensures efficient use of security resources.
