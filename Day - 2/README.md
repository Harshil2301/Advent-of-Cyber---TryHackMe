## ğŸ§  Key Concepts Covered

### ğŸ”¹ Social Engineering
Social engineering is the manipulation of individuals into performing actions or disclosing sensitive information by exploiting trust, urgency, fear, or curiosity.

Rather than attacking systems, attackers exploit **human psychology**.

---

### ğŸ”¹ Phishing Attacks
Phishing is a social engineering technique where attackers impersonate trusted entities to trick victims into:
- Revealing credentials  
- Clicking malicious links  
- Downloading harmful attachments  

Phishing commonly occurs via:
- Emails  
- Messages  
- Fake websites  

---

### ğŸ”¹ Why Phishing Is Effective
- Humans are often the weakest security link  
- Messages create urgency or fear  
- Attackers mimic legitimate communication convincingly  
- Technical defenses alone cannot fully prevent phishing

## ğŸ” Defensive Perspective (SOC View)
From a SOC standpoint, phishing detection involves:
- Monitoring email and message alerts  
- Identifying suspicious domains or links  
- Analyzing user-reported phishing attempts  
- Educating users to recognize phishing indicators  

Phishing prevention requires a **combination of technical controls and user awareness**.
